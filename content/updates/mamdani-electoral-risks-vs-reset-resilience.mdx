## 1. Why Mamdani's Method Risks Failure
### <JapaneseText>選挙の脆弱性</JapaneseText> · The Perils of Electoral Dependency

</ColoredSection>

Mamdani&apos;s strategy—bold taxes funding public goods—hinges on **institutional capture**, exposing it to layered reversals. Early indicators (November 5-9, 2025) portend trouble: Trump&apos;s funding freeze, Hochul&apos;s tentative tax approval, and billionaire exodus threats (e.g., Ackman/Bloomberg) could slash **$7.4 billion in federal aid**, crippling the $118 billion budget.

### The Three Dependencies: Achilles&apos; Heel of Electoral Socialism

**Mamdani&apos;s vulnerability** stems from three fatal dependencies that the **99% Reset** explicitly avoids:

**1. State cooperation required:** Hochul must approve NYC&apos;s tax surcharges. Precedent: She killed congestion pricing in 2024 despite legislative approval. **One veto collapses the $4B revenue model**. The Reset builds **credit unions and co-ops** that operate via private contracts—Alberta can&apos;t ban BC co-ops. No governor&apos;s signature required.

**2. Federal funding dependency:** NYC receives **$7.4B annually** from Washington. Trump&apos;s threat to cut &quot;communist&quot; cities is credible—he blocked sanctuary cities 2017-2020. Losing even 20% ($1.5B) forces service cuts. The Reset&apos;s **wealth funds** invest locally (housing, small business). Federal cuts to NYC don&apos;t touch Vancouver&apos;s **Vancity** deposits or Toronto&apos;s **CLTs**.

**3. Continuous electoral wins:** Mamdani needs 2026 (state legislature), 2028 (governor), and 2029 (reelection). **Losing any one reverses gains**. De Blasio won twice but his successor (Adams) undid reforms. The Reset&apos;s **co-op equity vests over 5-7 years**. Once workers own 51%, no mayor can &quot;un-own&quot; them. **CLT land leases run 99 years**—outlasting 20 election cycles.

### Why Nonviolent Movements Outlast Elections

Research (Chenoweth & Stephan, *Why Civil Resistance Works*, 2011) proves our strategy: **Nonviolent campaigns** succeed 53% of the time (1900-2006) versus violent campaigns at 26%. The **key factor**: broad participation (3.5% of population = tipping point).

**Reset advantage:** Bank boycotts, union drives, and **co-ops** require no violence—just withdrawal of consent. The **Montgomery Bus Boycott (1955-1956)** provides our blueprint: 40K Black residents boycotted for 381 days, costing the bus system $30K/day ($300K today). The Supreme Court ruled segregation unconstitutional in 1956. **Law changed via economic pressure, not votes.**

**Mamdani&apos;s risk:** Elections require 50%+1 every cycle. Boycotts need 10-20% sustained participation—an **easier threshold** that compounds over time.

### Historical Warning: De Blasio's Cautionary Tale

Bill de Blasio (NYC mayor 2014-2021) promised progressive transformation:
- **Win:** Universal pre-K (70K kids enrolled by 2015)
- **Stall:** Affordable housing (built 25K units, needed 200K)
- **Loss:** Police reform (BLM protests 2020; minimal change)

By 2021, approval fell to **32%**. Eric Adams (moderate Democrat) won, reversing: Pre-K expansion slowed (Cuomo shifted funding to state control), homeless sweeps resumed (undoing de Blasio&apos;s shelter expansions), and real estate got tax breaks (negating affordability gains).

**Lesson:** Electoral wins are sand castles. The next tide washes them away.

**Reset parallel:** Burlington VT&apos;s **CLT** (1984-present) survived **8 mayors** (4 conservative). Why? Legal trusts can&apos;t be repealed—only amended via supermajorities. **565 homes stayed affordable** through 40 years of political swings. This is **democratic socialist infrastructure** at its finest.

## 4. The Capital Capture Advantage
### <JapaneseText>資本の民主化</JapaneseText> · Ownership vs. Redistribution

</ColoredSection>

| Model | Flow of Wealth | Outcome |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Mamdani (Electoral)** | 1% → State → 99% (as wages/services) | State becomes new 1% (bureaucrat palaces) |
| **99% Reset (Ownership)** | 1% → **Citizens&apos; Wealth Fund** → 99% (as equity/dividends) | 99% become capitalist class ($2–4K/year per citizen) |

**Mamdani redistributes income** (via taxes) but leaves capital ownership unchanged: Billionaires still own firms (they just pay more), workers get services not shares, and the next mayor cuts taxes → services vanish.

**The Reset redistributes capital** (via funds/co-ops): **Citizens&apos; Wealth Fund** owns 40% of TSX by 2040, workers own **co-ops** (equity locked for 5-7 years), billionaires become minority shareholders, and it&apos;s **irreversible**—can&apos;t un-buy shares, can&apos;t dissolve vested equity.

**Example:** **Alaska Permanent Fund** (1976-present) holds $78B in oil assets, pays $1-2K/year dividend to all residents, and has survived **12 governors** (6 Republican, 6 Democrat). **Why?** Constitutional amendment requires statewide vote to repeal (never happened in 49 years). The Reset&apos;s wealth funds copy this model—**rooted in law, not politics**.
